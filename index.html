Manav (मानव) is a Sanskrit word meaning "human being," "man," or "mankind," symbolizing inherent human virtues like compassion, intelligence, and moral reasoning, often linked to the progenitor figure Manu in Hindu tradition. It's a popular name in Indian cultures, signifying humanity and a connection to ethical living.

  • Origin: Sanskrit
  • Meaning: Human, human being, man, mankind
  • Cultural Significance: Represents the pinnacle of creation with consciousness and the capacity for spiritual growth, often linked to the first man, Manu, in ancient texts like the Vedas and Upanishads
  • Connotations: Compassion, intelligence, righteousness, and universal human experience
