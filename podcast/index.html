Podcast format

“Building the world’s biggest entrepreneurship community.”

  • Intro (show the story highlights, numbers)
  • Company name and whether it’s venture backed
  • Explain the idea + value proposition
  • How did you think of the idea?
  • What was the first ugly version of your product?
  • Why did you want to solve this pain point?
  • How did you get your initial customers (reveal more numbers)
  • How did you hire the team?
  • Failures: setbacks, near-death moments, mistakes that cost a lot of money
  • Playbook for other founders
  • Tech stack: software tools you used
  • Numbers: revenue, growth, burn (optional)
  • What would you do differently today?

One sentence intro from Manav (example): “Today’s guest [Name] built a $4.2m SaaS without paid ads, without a sales team, and almost shut it down twice. Here’s exactly how.”

Manav (मानव) is a Sanskrit word meaning "human being," "man," or "mankind," symbolizing inherent human virtues like compassion, intelligence, and moral reasoning, often linked to the progenitor figure Manu in Hindu tradition. It's a popular name in Indian cultures, signifying humanity and a connection to ethical living.

  • Origin: Sanskrit
  • Meaning: Human, human being, man, mankind
  • Cultural Significance: Represents the pinnacle of creation with consciousness and the capacity for spiritual growth, often linked to the first man, Manu, in ancient texts like the Vedas and Upanishads
  • Connotations: Compassion, intelligence, righteousness, and universal human experience
